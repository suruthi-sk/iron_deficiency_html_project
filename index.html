Iron Deficiency in Tomato

Iron deficiency is a common issue in tomato plants, affecting their growth and fruit quality. This deficiency can lead to chlorosis, where the leaves turn yellow while veins remain green.

Important: Monitor your tomato plants for iron deficiency!

Symptoms of Iron Deficiency

  • Yellowing of young leaves
  • Stunted growth
  • Pale green foliage

Causes

  1. High soil pH
  2. Poor soil drainage
  3. Excessive phosphorus levels

Solutions

To treat iron deficiency in tomatoes, consider the following methods:

  • Applying chelated iron fertilizers
  • Adjusting soil pH
  • Improving drainage

Iron Levels in Tomato Plants

Growth Stage Iron Content (mg/kg)
Seedling 30
Vegetative 50
Fruit Development 80
